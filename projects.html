Terracore

Terracore is an upcoming turn based rpg being developed by myself and aspiring artist/musician Kryis.

It takes place in a future where earth has been exiled into the depths of the outer solar system, forcing humanity's decendants underground where the core's heat is just enough to keep them alive. Ren, recently waking up from cryosleep, now has to navigate this dark and unfamiliar world where survival won't be easy and the ones he once loved my not be as dead as he thought...

Any updates on it's development will be posted in the updates page of this site.

Personal UwU Extension

Personal UwU is a chrome extension I orginally made as a joke after my friend (Jared Barker) gave me $5 dollars to develop a "uwu chrome extension". But now is has over 150 users, 80% of which aren't even fellow Australians. So.... I guess this is a thing now.

The extension allows you to rub and pet a virtual 'uwu' after which it will make the uwu sound at a certain volume depending on how much you pet it. Thats it. Thats all it does

It is open source and has a github page, so do with that what you will. If I ever decide to update it, you will find info on that in the updates page

Box Battleground Series

Back in grade 9, there was a game making competition at my school. And this was the winning game. Probably because everyone else made theirs in scratch...

Box Battlegrounds is a series of games that involve two players battling it out on the same machine. The goal is to reduce your opponents size, while collecting orbs to increase your own size and eventually knock them out. Each round also introduces some variation to spice up the gameplay. This includes realistic gravity, black holes and a counter strike flashbang?

The first game is the one made for the competition and is written using pygame and python. The second one, made more recently, is developed using lua and the LOVE framework and includes better performance, more round variations and generally more polish
You can find both the first game and the second game on their github pages
